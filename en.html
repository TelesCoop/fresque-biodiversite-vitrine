The Biodiversity Collage

Discover, through a fun and collaborative workshop, the systemic aspects of biodiversity: what it is, what it enables and what degrades it.

Participate!

Why the Biodiversity Collage?

To understand the challenges and pressures around biodiversity, through a fun, collaborative, visual and accessible workshop, based on the IPBES report.

"The global rate of species extinction is already at least tens to hundreds of times higher than the average rate over the past 10 million years and is accelerating."

IPBES, 2019

How does the Biodiversity Collage work?

Divided into groups of 5-7, recreate an ecosystem and discover the impacts of human disturbance within it. Find the links between the 39 cards, distributed in 5 lots, to understand what biodiversity is, what it enables and what degrades it. Decorate and find a title for the Collage in order to present your work to the other groups. Reflect on learnings and collectively develop an action plan to stop biodiversity loss. The debrief is adapted to the objectives of each workshop.

The story of the Collage

2019

Development, user testing & scientific review

After developing the tool, we tested it with more than 100 people and with experts from the National Museum of Natural History, the French Biodiversity Agency, IUCN, Humanity & Biodiversity, and business decision makers.

1200 students played!

As part of the biodiversity week at the Leonardo da Vinci University center where 50 trained facilitators supervised this giant workshop.

2020

More scientific validation and online workshops

During the confinement, more than 150 people participated in online sessions from various companies. Employees can still do the Collage as part of their internal training programs.

Wider dissemination on the model of the Climate Fresk.

The idea is to entrust distribution to the general public in return for usage rights to the association.

2021-2023

Biodiversity Collage at the Nature Congress in Marseille and at COP15 in Montreal.

Launch of the workshop internationally (Belgium, Switzerland, Spain, Canada ...).
